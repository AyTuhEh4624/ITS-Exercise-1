ULTRAKILL is a fast-paced ultraviolent old school first-person shooter developed by Arsi "Hakita" Patala and published by New Blood Interactive. You play as V1, a combat machine fueled by blood who has ventured into the depths of Hell after the extinction of humanity. Hell abounds with demons and tormented souls, sources of blood that you must rend apart in order to refuel yourself and survive.

ULTRAKILL's gameplay combines features from classic shooters like Quake, modern shooters like Doom (2016) and character action games like Devil May Cry. The game places emphasis on movement, dodging attacks, and efficient gunplay in its arena-style levels. In addition, the player's health can only be restored by showering in the blood of enemies, requiring the player to adopt an aggressive playstyle that regularly places them in close combat with their opponents.

Dual Wield powerups normally last only 30 seconds, but the secret Dual Wield Orb in [ 4-2: GOD DAMN THE SUN ] and the lone Dual Wield spawn in [ 5-3: SHIP OF FOOLS ] have doubled 60-second durations, and the Dual Wield at the end of [ 4-1: SLAVES TO POWER ] has a quadrupled 120-second duration.

A Sentry can be chargebacked, causing its bullet to Ricoshot, dealing 43 damage to any target it hits.

The most damaging projectile from any enemy is the Hideous Mass mortar, dealing a whopping 60 damage on hit.

The Sharpshooter Revolver's alternate fire can detonate both parried Shotgun shells and V2's Piercer Revolver shots.

The Leviathan's lunge, despite being telegraphed as an unparriable, can be parried, although it's very difficult and will not cancel its attack.

During Seasonal Events, characters in cutscenes will also wear seasonally-appropriate headgear.

Flesh Panopticon's eyes have a strange interaction with the Whiplash, taking 26 grapples to kill a Golden Eye (5.2 Damage), which would imply they have a little over 5 Health, but using any other source of damage to deal exactly 5 Damage results in their demise.

V1, despite their small stature, is considered one of the most dangerous machines even amongst weak-minded enemies in the game. All enemies, even if they are on opposing factions, all target V1 simply because they believe it to the be most powerful thing in the room... They aren't wrong.
